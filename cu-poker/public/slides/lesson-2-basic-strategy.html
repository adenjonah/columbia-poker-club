Basic Strategy

Four ideas that turn you from a losing beginner into a winning one.

↓ use arrow keys or spacebar

Why beginners lose

Three leaks cost everything

Too many hands

Playing 50% of hands means entering most pots with trash. Trash loses over time.

Too passive

Checking and calling lets opponents see free cards. Betting builds pots and folds hands.

Ignoring position

Playing the same hand the same way from every seat is a million-dollar mistake.

Fix these three and you'll beat most home games without opening a book.

Out of position

You act before your opponent on every street. You show information first. Harder.

In position

You act after your opponent. You see their action first. Easier. More profitable.

Rule of thumb: play more hands late, fewer early.

Value

If you have the best hand, betting gets more chips in from worse hands.

Fold equity

Even weak, a bet can win right now if they fold. Calling never folds anyone out.

Idea 4 of 4

Bet sizing, simplified

Preflop open

2.5–3× BB

Standard size. Bigger from early, smaller from late.

Continuation bet

33–50% pot

Small on dry boards. Larger on wet boards with draws.

Value bet (river)

66–100% pot

Confident you're ahead? Charge them.

Red flag: very small = "please call." Very big on river = nuts or bluff.

Check your understanding
You open-raise preflop and one player calls. Flop: K♦ 7♣ 2♠ — a dry, disconnected board. You have A♠ K♠. Best continuation-bet sizing?
Right. On a dry, disconnected board there are few draws to charge. A small c-bet (33%) gets value from worse Kings and can fold out middle pairs cheaply.
Checking lets your opponent realize equity for free. Bet.
Overbetting a dry board mostly folds out worse hands and inflates the pot against better ones.
Shoving for 50× the pot won't get called by worse.
Leaks to avoid

Beginner mistake checklist

  • Limping — calling the big blind preflop. Either raise or fold.
  • Slow-playing monsters — bet sets, straights, flushes. You'll get called more than you think.
  • Calling "just to see" — you need a reason. "Curiosity" isn't one.
  • Chasing with bad odds — drawing needs math. Covered in Lesson 3.
  • Playing tilted — losing a hand doesn't make the next hand a better call.
  • Not paying attention — who raised? From where? Information at the table is free.
Check your understanding
You open from UTG with A♠ A♥. The button 3-bets you. The small blind cold-4-bets all-in for 200 big blinds. The button folds. What's the worst play?
Right. Folding Aces preflop against a single opponent is almost always a disaster — you're favored against every other starting hand. Snap call.
Aces are the best preflop hand. Against one opponent you should never fold them preflop.
Recap

Four ideas, in order

  1. Play fewer, better hands. Fold trash. Raise premiums.
  2. Use position. Play more from the button, less from UTG.
  3. Be the bettor. Raising and betting beat calling. Calling never folds anyone out.
  4. Size with purpose. Dry boards small. Wet boards bigger. River value biggest.
