Setup

Your 7 cards

2 hole cards — private

+

5 community cards — shared

7 total cards → pick your best 5

You don't have to use both hole cards. You can use 2, 1, or even 0.

Everyone gets 2 private cards, called hole cards. Then 5 cards are dealt face-up in the center, shared by all players. From those 7 total, you pick the best possible 5-card hand. You don't have to use both hole cards.

Poker has exactly two ways to win. First: have the best hand when cards are compared. Here, pocket Aces plus two more Aces on the board makes four of a kind — Quad Aces. Second: bet in a way that makes everyone else give up. That second path is what separates poker from most card games.

  • High Card17.4%

  • Straight4.62%
  • Flush3.03%
  • Full House2.60%
  • Four of a Kind0.17%
  • Straight Flush0.028%

One pair shows up almost 44% of the time. Two pair another 23%. Together those two make up two-thirds of all hands. Everything from three of a kind up is under 5%.

Actions

Your five options

The available actions depend on whether someone has already bet.

No bet before you

Check
Pass without betting. Stay in for free.
Bet
Put chips in first. Others must now respond.

You can fold here, but why give up a free card?

Bet or blind before you

Fold
Give up. Out of this pot.
Call
Match the current bet to stay in.
Raise
Increase the bet. Force others to decide again.

Every decision you'll ever make in poker comes down to these five.

Five choices. When nobody has bet yet: check or bet. When someone has already bet or you're facing a blind: fold, call, or raise. You CAN fold when nobody has bet, but you'd be giving up a free card.
Betting Rules

How much can you bet?

This is No Limit Hold'em. There's no cap on bet size.

Minimum bet Equal to the big blind. In a 1/2 game, min bet = 2. Minimum raise At least the size of the previous bet or raise on top. Maximum raise All-in — push your entire stack. No limit means no cap.

Example — 1/2 game:

Player A bets 10

Player B min-raises to 20 (raise of 10 on top)

Player C min-raises to 30 (raise of 10 on top)

Player D could raise to anything ≥ 40 …or shove all-in

No Limit means there's no cap on how much you can bet. The minimum bet is the big blind. When raising, you have to raise by at least the size of the previous bet or raise. So if someone bets 10, you can raise to 20 or more — the raise amount is at least 10. The maximum? Everything you have. That's all-in.

The fold win

6 players. Alice deals. Bob (SB), Carol (BB). You, Dave, Eve.

Preflop: You fold. Dave raises. Eve folds. Alice folds. Bob folds. Carol calls.

Flop: J♠ 8♥ 4♣  Carol checks. Dave bets. Carol calls.

Turn: Q♦  Carol checks. Dave bets big. Carol calls.

River: 6♣  Carol checks. Dave shoves all-in.

Carol: Fold

Dave wins. Cards are never shown.

Dave might have had the nuts. Dave might have had nothing.
When everyone folds, the winner never has to reveal. That ambiguity is what makes poker interesting.
6 players start. You fold preflop with a weak hand. By the river it's Dave vs Carol. Dave shoves all-in. Carol folds. Dave wins without ever showing cards. Could have been a monster hand. Could have been a bluff. That's poker.

Recap

What you now know

1. Best 5 of 7 cards wins. 2 hole cards + 5 community.

2. 10 hand rankings. Pair and two pair decide most pots.

3. Blinds create action. The button rotates so everyone pays.

4. Four betting rounds: preflop, flop, turn, river.

5. Five actions: fold, check, call, bet, raise.

6. You can win by making everyone else fold. No cards shown.

Fold Check Call Bet Raise
Six things. Best 5 of 7. Know the common hand rankings. Blinds rotate. Four betting rounds. Five possible actions. And you can win without the best hand if everyone folds. That's the complete ruleset.

Cheat Sheet

Hand Rankings (best to worst)
  • Royal Flush — A K Q J 10, same suit
  • Straight Flush — 5 in a row, same suit
  • Four of a Kind — 4 of same rank
  • Full House — 3 of a kind + pair
  • Flush — 5 same suit
  • Straight — 5 in a row
  • Three of a Kind — 3 of same rank
  • Two Pair — 2 different pairs
  • One Pair — 2 of same rank
  • High Card — nothing, best card plays
Betting Actions
  • Fold   Give up, out of the hand
  • Check   Pass (only if no bet yet)
  • Call   Match the current bet
  • Bet   First chips in
  • Raise   Increase someone's bet

Game Flow
  • Preflop — 2 hole cards, bet from left of BB
  • Flop — 3 board cards, bet from left of Dealer
  • Turn — 4th card, same as flop
  • River — 5th card, same as flop
  • Showdown — Best 5 of 7 wins

When to take each action

Bet You think you have the best hand and want to get paid (value), or you think you can make others fold (bluff). Raise Same reasons as betting, but stronger. You're very confident in your hand or your bluff. Call You think you have a decent chance of winning on later streets. You're not ready to give up, but not strong enough to raise. Fold Your hand is weak and the price is too high. Save your chips for a better spot.

When in doubt: if your hand is strong, bet. If it's weak, fold. Calling is the middle ground — use it when you have a reason to see more cards.

Now let's play.

Quick strategy overview before we play. Pairs and big cards are the best starting hands. When deciding what to do: bet when you think you're ahead or can make others fold. Raise for the same reasons but when you're more confident. Call when you have a reasonable chance on later streets. Fold when you're beat. This is just a starting framework — real strategy gets much deeper.
