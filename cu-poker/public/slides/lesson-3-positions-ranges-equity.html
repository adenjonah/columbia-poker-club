The four pillars

Every decision is one of four questions

Where am I sitting?

Position — early, middle, late, or blinds. Decides how many hands you can profitably play.

What could they have?

Range — not a single hand, but a set of hands consistent with their actions.

What's the price?

Pot odds — how much you must call relative to what's already there.

How often do I win?

Equity — your hand's win percentage vs. their range.

Big idea: if equity > price, call. Otherwise fold.

Pillar 2 · Ranges

Think in sets, not single hands

You can't see their cards — so don't fixate on one holding. Reason about a range: the plausible hands consistent with every action so far.

Beginner

"I think they have KQ."

Fixates on one hand. Usually wrong.

Right instinct

"Their 3-bet range is about QQ+, AK, maybe AQs."

Now you can do math against a set.

A BTN opening range

~40% of hands

13×13 hand matrix. Diagonal = pairs. Above = suited. Below = offsuit. Gold = in range.

All pairs, broadways, suited Aces and Kings, suited connectors, and many offsuit Broadways. Position lets you play wide.

Check your understanding
An opponent opens from UTG (first to act preflop). Which of these is most likely in their range?
Right. UTG opens a tight range — big pairs and strong broadways. AK is a classic UTG open.
Speculative hands are BTN/CO territory, not UTG.
UTG opens tight. Junk hands don't make the cut.

You need to win at least 25% of the time for calling to break even.

Shortcuts to memorize

Four common spots

Bigger bet → more equity needed. Worth memorizing all four.

Check your understanding
Pot is 200. Your opponent bets 100 on the turn. How much equity do you need to break even on a call?
Correct. 100 / (200 + 100 + 100) = 100 / 400 = 25%. A half-pot bet always gives 25% pot odds — memorize.
Formula: call / (pot after your call) = 100 / 400 = 25%. Half-pot always gives 25%.
Pillar 4 · Equity

How often do I win?

Your hand: A♠ 4♠
K♠ 7♠ 2♦

Two spades in your hand, two on the board. 9 spades left in the deck = 9 outs to a flush.

Rule of 2 & 4: outs × 4 on the flop (two cards to come)
9 × 4 ≈ 36% equity by the river

Turn (1 card left)

outs × 2 = equity %

Flop (2 cards left)

outs × 4 = equity %

Putting it together

Equity vs. price

Equity > required

Call.

You win often enough to justify the price.

Equity < required

Fold.

You're paying too much for how often you win.

Everything else — reads, bluffs, sizing — sits on top of this single comparison.

Check your understanding
Flop: K♠ 7♠ 2♦. You hold A♠ 4♠ — nut flush draw (9 outs). Pot 100, opponent bets 50. Call or fold?
Right. Required: 50 / 200 = 25%. Your equity: 9 × 4 ≈ 36%. 36% > 25% — call. Plus you have implied odds when you hit.
9 outs × 4 ≈ 36%. Price is 25%. You have more equity than the price demands — call.
"No made hand" is a beginner reflex. A strong draw at the right price is a call.
Recap

Four pillars, one decision

  1. Position — play wider late, tighter early.
  2. Ranges — reason about sets, not a single hand.
  3. Pot odds — call / (pot + call) = required equity.
  4. Equity — outs × 4 on flop, × 2 on turn. Compare to required.

Memorize: ½ pot = 25%, ⅔ pot = 29%, full = 33%.
