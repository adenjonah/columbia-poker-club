The four pillars

Every decision is one of four questions

Where am I sitting?

Position — early, middle, late, or blinds. Decides how many hands you can profitably play.

What could they have?

Range — not a single hand, but a set of hands consistent with their actions.

What's the price?

Pot odds — how much you must call relative to what's already there.

How often do I win?

Equity — your hand's win percentage vs. their range.

The big idea: if your equity is greater than the price, you call. Otherwise, fold.

Early (UTG / UTG+1)

7+ players still to act. Many chances to get a better hand behind you. Play tight.

Middle (MP / HJ)

Open up a little. Position postflop will usually be decent but not great.

Late (CO / BTN)

Few players behind. Last to act postflop. Play the widest ranges here.

BTN plays roughly 3× more hands than UTG. Same cards, different seat, entirely different decision.

Pillar 2 · Ranges

Think in sets, not single hands

You can't see their cards, so you shouldn't fixate on one holding. Instead, reason about a range: the plausible hands that fit every action so far.

Beginner mistake

"I think they have KQ."

Fixates on one hand. Usually wrong.

Right instinct

"Their 3-bet range is roughly QQ+, AK, maybe AQs."

Now you can do math against a set of hands.

A BTN opening range

~40% of hands

Rows = first card. Columns = second card. Gold = opened. Top-left to bottom-right diagonal = pairs.

All pairs, all broadways, most suited aces, suited connectors, and plenty of offsuit Aces and Kings. Position lets you play wide.

Right. UTG opens a tight range — mostly big pairs and strong broadways. AK is a classic UTG open.

Pillar 3 · Pot odds

What's the price?

When you face a bet, pot odds tell you how often you need to win to break even.

The formula

required equity = call / (pot + call)

Example: pot is 100, opponent bets 50. You have to call 50 into a pot of 150.

50 / (150 + 50) = 50 / 200 = 25%

If you'll win at least 25% of the time, calling is at worst break-even.

Shortcuts to memorize

Four common spots

They bet ½ potneed 25%
They bet ⅔ potneed 29%
They bet full potneed 33%
They bet 2× potneed 40%

Bigger bet → you need more equity to call. Intuitive, but worth memorizing the four.

Check your understanding
Pot is 200. Your opponent bets 100 on the turn. How much equity do you need to break even on a call?
Correct. 100 / (200 + 100 + 100) = 100 / 400 = 25%. A half-pot bet always gives you 25% pot odds — memorize this.
Formula: call / (pot after your call) = 100 / (200 + 100 + 100) = 100 / 400 = 25%. A half-pot bet always gives 25%.
Pillar 4 · Equity

How often do I win?

Equity = the % of the time your hand beats your opponent's range by showdown.

Made hand equity

You already have something. E.g. top pair vs. a random hand is ~70%.

Draw equity — the Rule of 2 and 4

On the turn, multiply outs × 2 for your equity %.
On the flop (two cards to come), multiply outs × 4.

Example: 9 outs for a flush on the flop → 9 × 4 ≈ 36% equity to hit by the river.

Putting it together

Equity vs. price

Equity > required

Call.

You win often enough to justify the price.

Equity < required

Fold.

You're paying too much for how often you'll win.

Everything else — reading opponents, bluffing, bet sizing — is built on top of this single comparison.

Check your understanding
Flop: K♠ 7♠ 2♦. You hold A♠ 4♠ — a nut flush draw (9 outs). Pot is 100, opponent bets 50. Call or fold?
Right. Required equity: 50 / 200 = 25%. Your equity: 9 outs × 4 ≈ 36%. Equity (36%) > price (25%), so call. Plus you have implied odds if you hit.
9 outs × 4 ≈ 36% equity. Required is 25%. You have more equity than the price demands — it's a call.
"No made hand" is a beginner reflex. A strong draw with correct pot odds is a call.
Recap

Four pillars, one decision

  1. Position — play wider late, tighter early.
  2. Ranges — reason about sets of hands, not one specific hand.
  3. Pot odds — call / (pot + call) = required equity.
  4. Equity — outs × 4 on flop, × 2 on turn. Compare to required.

Memorize: ½ pot = 25%, ⅔ pot = 29%, full pot = 33%.
