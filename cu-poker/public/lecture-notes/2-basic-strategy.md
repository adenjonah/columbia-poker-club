# Basic Strategy

The four ideas that make you a winning beginner at NLHE.

## Why beginners lose

Three leaks explain the majority of beginner losses:

1. **Playing too many hands** — entering pots with trash loses money over time.
2. **Passive play** — checking and calling lets opponents realize equity for free.
3. **Ignoring position** — playing the same hand the same way from every seat is a huge mistake.

## Idea 1: Play fewer, better hands

### Premium (always play)
AA, KK, QQ, JJ, AK

### Strong (play from most seats)
TT, 99, AQ, AJs, KQs

### Speculative (late position, cheap entry)
Suited connectors, small pairs, suited aces

### Trash (fold)
J4o, 92o, 73o, K3o, Q5o — unsuited, disconnected, not premium.

## Idea 2: Position is money

- **Out of position (OOP)**: you act first on every street. You give information first. Harder.
- **In position (IP)**: you act after your opponent. You see their action first. Easier, more profitable.

**Rule of thumb**: play more hands from late position (CO, BTN), fewer from early (UTG, UTG+1).

## Idea 3: Bet, don't call

Raising and betting beat calling and checking for two reasons:

- **Value**: if your hand is good, betting gets more chips in from worse hands.
- **Fold equity**: even with a weak hand, a bet can win the pot right now. Calling never folds anyone out.

Don't be a **calling station** (calls too much, rarely raises) — they're the easiest players to beat.

## Idea 4: Bet sizing

| Spot | Typical size |
|------|--------------|
| Preflop open | 2.5–3× BB |
| Continuation bet | 33–50% pot |
| Value bet on river | 66–100% pot |

- **Dry boards** (no draws): smaller c-bets work.
- **Wet boards** (straights/flushes possible): size up to charge draws.
- **River**: bigger bets = more confident.

## Beginner mistakes to avoid

- **Limping** (just calling the BB preflop) — either raise or fold.
- **Slow-playing monsters** — bet big hands. You get called more than you think.
- **Calling "just to see"** — need a reason. Curiosity isn't one.
- **Chasing bad odds** — drawing needs math (Lesson 3).
- **Playing tilted** — losing doesn't make the next hand a better call.
- **Not paying attention** — information at the table is free.

## Recap

1. Play fewer, better hands.
2. Use position.
3. Be the bettor, not the caller.
4. Size with purpose.
