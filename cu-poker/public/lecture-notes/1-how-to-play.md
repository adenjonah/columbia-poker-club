# No Limit Hold'em: Rules & Game Flow

A 30-minute beginner lesson covering rules, game flow, and the combinatorial math behind hand rankings. No strategy — strategy is covered in later lessons.

## Two Ways to Win

1. **Best hand at showdown** — your 5-card hand beats everyone who stayed in
2. **Everyone folds** — bet in a way that makes others give up; no cards shown

## Your 7 Cards

- **2 hole cards** dealt face-down to you
- **5 community cards** dealt face-up in the center, shared by all players
- Make the best 5-card hand using any combination of the 7 (you don't have to use both hole cards)

## The Deck

Standard 52-card deck: 4 suits, 13 ranks each. In Hold'em, **suits have no ranking**. Ace is highest, but can also be low in the A-2-3-4-5 straight (the only exception).

## Hand Rankings (best to worst)

**Rare:**
1. Royal Flush — A, K, Q, J, 10 same suit
2. Straight Flush — 5 in sequence, same suit
3. Four of a Kind
4. Full House
5. Flush

**Common (these decide most pots):**
6. Straight
7. Three of a Kind
8. Two Pair
9. One Pair — most common winning hand
10. High Card

## The Math

- **7 choose 5 = 21** different 5-card hands possible from your 7 cards
- Over **133 million** unique 7-card combinations exist across all deals

### Hand probabilities

| Hand | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| One Pair | ~44% |
| Two Pair | ~23% |
| High Card | ~17% |
| Three of a Kind, Straight, Flush, etc. | <5% combined |
| Royal Flush | 1 in ~30,000 |

Calibrate expectations around the common hands — not the rare ones.

## The Table

- 6–9 players around an oval
- Community cards in the middle
- **Dealer button** (white disc) marks the "dealer" position; rotates one seat clockwise each hand
- Everything about who pays and who acts first is defined relative to the button

## Blinds

Before cards are dealt, two seats post forced bets:
- **Small Blind** — half the minimum, seat immediately left of button
- **Big Blind** — full minimum, next seat left

Blinds rotate each hand so every player pays equally over a full orbit.

## The Five Actions

- **Fold** — give up your hand
- **Check** — pass without betting (only if nobody bet yet)
- **Call** — match the current bet
- **Bet** — put chips in first
- **Raise** — increase someone else's bet

## Anatomy of a Hand (5 stages)

1. **Preflop** — 2 hole cards dealt, betting round. Action starts LEFT OF THE BIG BLIND.
2. **Flop** — 3 community cards, betting round. Action starts LEFT OF THE DEALER (this trips people up).
3. **Turn** — 1 more community card, betting round.
4. **River** — final community card, final betting round.
5. **Showdown** — remaining players reveal hands; best 5-card hand wins. If everyone folds first, no showdown needed.

## Key Pitfalls

- **Betting-order change postflop** — preflop starts left of BB; flop/turn/river start left of dealer.
- **Best 5 of 7 trap** — with A-A-K-K-Q on the board, a player holding two Kings has **four of a kind** (beats any full house). Always check the full 7.
- **Suits never break ties** — same hand ranks = split pot.

## Common Questions

**Can you use just one hole card?** Yes. Use 2, 1, or 0 of your hole cards — whatever makes the best 5.

**What if two players have the same hand?** Split pot. Suits don't break ties.

**What's the minimum raise?** At least the size of the previous bet or raise.

**What does "No Limit" mean?** You can bet any amount up to all your chips at any time.

## Recap

1. Best 5 of 7
2. Know the common rankings
3. Blinds rotate each hand
4. Four betting rounds: preflop, flop, turn, river
5. Five actions: fold, check, call, bet, raise
6. You can win without showing cards
