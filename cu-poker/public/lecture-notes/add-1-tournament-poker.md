# How to Play Tournament Poker

## Tournament Structure

### Buy-ins and Payouts
- **Buy-in**: Entry fee to join the tournament
- **Prize Pool**: Total money distributed to winners
- **Payout Structure**: How prize money is distributed
- **Bubble**: Last place before money (ITM - In The Money)

### Blind Levels
- **Blind Structure**: How blinds increase over time
- **Ante**: Additional forced bet in later levels
- **Stack-to-Blind Ratio**: Your stack size relative to blinds
- **M-ratio**: Harrington's M-ratio for stack assessment

## Tournament Phases

### Early Stage
- **Deep stacks**: 100+ big blinds
- **Conservative play**: Avoid big confrontations
- **Build chips**: Accumulate without risking elimination
- **Table image**: Establish tight-aggressive image

### Middle Stage
- **Medium stacks**: 20-100 big blinds
- **Selective aggression**: Pick spots carefully
- **Bubble play**: Avoid elimination before money
- **Steal blinds**: Use position to steal pots

### Late Stage
- **Short stacks**: <20 big blinds
- **Push/fold strategy**: All-in or fold decisions
- **Final table**: Adjust for pay jumps
- **Heads-up**: One-on-one play

## ICM (Independent Chip Model)

### Definition
- **ICM**: Mathematical model for tournament equity
- **Chip value**: Not linear - chips worth less as stack grows
- **Bubble factor**: How much more valuable chips become near bubble

### Applications
- **Fold equity**: Value of forcing opponents to fold
- **Pay jump considerations**: Risk vs reward near pay jumps
- **Final table play**: Adjust strategy for payouts

## Tournament-Specific Concepts

### Stack Sizes
- **Big Stack**: 2x+ average stack
- **Average Stack**: Around average chip count
- **Short Stack**: <20 big blinds
- **Micro Stack**: <10 big blinds

### Bubble Play
- **Bubble**: Last place before money
- **Tight play**: Avoid elimination
- **Loose play**: Exploit tight opponents
- **Bubble factor**: Increased fold equity

### Final Table Strategy
- **Pay jumps**: Significant money differences
- **Risk management**: Balance aggression with survival
- **Opponent reads**: Adjust to final table dynamics
- **Heads-up play**: Completely different strategy

## Key Differences from Cash Games

### No Rebuy
- **Elimination**: Cannot rebuy if eliminated
- **Risk management**: More conservative approach
- **Stack preservation**: Avoid unnecessary risks

### Increasing Blinds
- **Time pressure**: Must accumulate chips
- **Stack-to-blind ratio**: Changes throughout tournament
- **Adaptation**: Adjust strategy as blinds increase

### Prize Structure
- **Non-linear payouts**: Top-heavy prize distribution
- **Pay jumps**: Significant money differences
- **Risk vs reward**: Balance aggression with survival
